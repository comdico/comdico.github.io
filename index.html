About Me

I have worked as a Web Professional for most of my professional career. Although I remember being able to surf the entire web in one sitting, the past couple of years have been the most exciting: Responsive Design and HTML 5 have emphasized the web's roots in semantic code and accessibility while the many frameworks and libraries have opened up new creative possibilities.

My background is in the Liberal Arts (M.A. in Creative Writing) where I learned skills that have proven very handy working on the web: critical reading and interpretation (turning requirements into products), understanding narrative structures (storymapping and user behavior), and the group workshop critique (managing creative teams). The latter is especially relevant in our agile, collaborative workspace!

In my spare time I enjoy photography, filmmaking, and music - endeavors which keep my creative well full and provide fresh insights into the creative process - as well as keeping me abreast of the unfolding convergence of digital media.

Websites tell stories about people, brands, and organizations. Authentic stories have the power to move us. So we build the experience collaboratively, from the inside out: Vision, Content, and Mobile first.

In the heat of a busy project, with rushed schedules, tight budgets, and complex requirements, it is all-too easy to lose sight of the Big Picture. Effective User Experience Design maintains a project's original vision and sees it through to final delivery.

I believe that User Experience Design can, and should, retain an artisanal approach, one where the spine of the product is not only minimally viable, but also deeply cohesive. However, the key that unlocks a project's true potential is collaboration.

There is a proven methodology to achieve high quality results with such an approach - and is used to produce most motion pictures. Interestingly, our industry's techniques, however less glamorous, turn out to be very similar to filmmaking.

For example:

Personas : Cast
Waypoints : Acts
Usability Test : Screen Test
Wireframe : Script
Prototype : Storyboard
Scenarios : Backstory
Storymap : Plot
End Users : Audience
Collaboration : Collaboration

Recent Work:

The Baby Safety Zone

Babysafety Zone

babysafetyzone.org

Consumer-based website advocating for baby safety. Design used bold colors, including gender-based pink and blue, while retaining a modern feel. I chose to use Story Mapping for the site's spine and initial backlog in order to retain coherency to the project, which included plans for future mobile app integration.

Society for Biomaterials

Society for Biomaterials

biomaterials.org

Website for Scientific Professional Society. Research indicated that what drew members to the organization was it's unique culture that emphasized comraderie. Website imagery was designed to draw users in and then showcase real members. Responsive menu was an original design that borrowed from current responsive patterns but retained iconography to help guide users unfamilar with the pattern.

The Osteoarthritis Research Society International

The Osteoarthritis Research Society International

oarsi.org

Website for an international Medical Society with a responsive design using Drupal 7 CMS. Research indicated that members highly valued the scientific research contained within the site but found it difficult to find. We built a Content Library that centralized the content and used Drupal Taxonomy for sorting - and for future content personalization.
